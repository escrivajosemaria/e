Overview

Vaccination and immunization are among the most effective public health interventions ever developed. Vaccines work by training the immune system to recognize and fight diseases before they cause serious illness or death. Today, vaccines exist for more than 30 life-threatening diseases, including diphtheria, tetanus, pertussis, measles, polio, hepatitis B, yellow fever, meningitis, and human papillomavirus (HPV). Immunization currently prevents an estimated 3.5 to 5 million deaths every year and has saved at least 154 million lives over the past 50 years, the majority of them infants. World Health Organization ↗

Despite these monumental successes, global immunization coverage remains uneven. In 2024, around 14.3 million children worldwide did not receive even a single basic vaccine — commonly referred to as zero-dose children — highlighting persistent access and equity gaps. World Health Organization ↗ Routine coverage for key vaccines such as DTP3 hovered around 85 percent globally, while coverage for yellow fever in at-risk countries was only about 52 percent. Continued investment and strategic action are essential to achieve the goals of the Immunization Agenda 2030 ↗.

What I Do

Areas of Practice

Combining technical planning, data-informed decision-making, and community engagement to improve coverage, reduce preventable disease risk, and ensure immunization services reach underserved populations.

01

Routine Immunization Programs

Strengthening planning, delivery, and integration of routine immunization services to improve consistency, equity, and system performance across facilities and communities.

02

Coverage Surveys & Assessments

Supporting the design and use of coverage assessments to identify immunization gaps, understand missed populations, and inform targeted, data-driven interventions.

03

Disease Prevention Strategy

Contributing to evidence-informed prevention strategies that align immunization efforts with broader public health priorities and epidemiological trends.

04

Community Engagement & Demand

Promoting approaches that build trust, address vaccine hesitancy, and strengthen community participation to improve uptake and sustained use of immunization services.

05

Health System Coordination

Working with government bodies, partners, and community structures to support aligned planning, efficient implementation, and sustainable disease prevention outcomes.

06

Capacity Building

Building technical and operational capacity across health system levels to sustain high-quality immunization delivery and disease prevention programming.

Core Focus

Focus Areas

Routine Immunization

Supporting the planning, coordination, and delivery of routine vaccination services within primary health care systems to ensure continuity, equity, and quality of care. The World Health Organization recognizes routine immunization as the most effective and equitable way to protect populations from vaccine-preventable diseases.

Coverage Surveys & Monitoring

Designing and supporting vaccination coverage assessments and monitoring systems to identify underserved populations and geographic gaps. Reliable data is essential for informed decision-making and guides planning, resource targeting, and accountability. World Health Organization

Disease Prevention Strategies

Contributing to the development and implementation of disease prevention strategies informed by epidemiological evidence and public health priorities to reduce the burden of vaccine-preventable diseases and protect community health.

Community Engagement & Demand

Working with communities, local leaders, and frontline actors to strengthen trust in vaccination services. UNICEF highlights the importance of listening to communities, addressing concerns, and ensuring services are culturally appropriate and responsive to local needs.
