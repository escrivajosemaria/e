The Challenge

Moving Beyond Vertical Interventions

Effective health programming requires a fundamental shift from disease-specific siloes to integrated system strengthening. Programmes that address one problem in isolation rarely produce lasting change.

I work at the intersection of policy design and frontline delivery — mapping the real constraints that prevent good intentions from becoming good outcomes.

Systems Thinking

Mapping Interconnection

Sustainable impact requires understanding how supply chains, policy environments, workforce dynamics, and human behavior interact — and where small changes produce outsized results.

I bring a structured analytical lens to health programs, identifying feedback loops and systemic bottlenecks that conventional assessments overlook.

04
Process

How I Work

Step 01

Assess

Deep-dive contextual root-cause analysis and stakeholder mapping to identify systemic bottlenecks that affect programme performance across all levels.

Step 02

Co-Design

Collaborative strategy building with local leaders and frontline actors to ensure ownership, cultural alignment, and operational feasibility from day one.

Step 03

Implement

Phased rollout with heavy focus on quality improvement cycles, frontline empowerment, and rapid feedback mechanisms that prevent early failure.

Step 04

Monitor

Real-time learning loops that allow rapid pivoting, early gap detection, and transparent performance reporting to stakeholders at every level.

Step 05

Scale

Evidence-based expansion and permanent policy integration within the national health system — turning project pilots into enduring infrastructure.
