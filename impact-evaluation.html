Overview

Evaluation and impact assessment are critical to understanding whether health programs are achieving their intended outcomes and delivering value to the populations they serve. Effective evaluation goes beyond measuring activities and outputs — it examines relevance, effectiveness, efficiency, and contribution to change, providing decision-makers with evidence that informs learning, accountability, and program improvement.

Approach

Work here is grounded in applied public health and development practice, where methodological rigour must be balanced with operational realities. The focus is on credible, decision-relevant evidence that supports adaptive management, strengthens accountability, and improves health system performance.

Core Principle

Evidence is most influential when designed with use in mind — from the first question to the final report. Evaluation is approached as a learning and decision-support function, not a compliance exercise.

Philosophy

Evidence Designed
for Use

Evaluation is approached as a learning and decision-support function rather than a compliance exercise. Strong evaluations begin with clarity on why evidence is needed, who will use it, and how it will inform action. This perspective reflects widely accepted principles in utilization-focused evaluation.

Methodological rigour, ethical standards, and contextual relevance are treated as equally important. Evaluation designs prioritize feasibility, data quality, and transparency, recognizing that overly complex methodologies can undermine both data credibility and practical use in real-world health programs.

Framework

Four Stages of
Rigorous Design

Evaluations are structured using clear frameworks that link program objectives to measurable results.

01
Framework Design
Evaluation questions, criteria, and indicators are defined at the outset to align with program goals, stakeholder priorities, and reporting requirements. Theories of change clarify assumptions.
02
Data Collection
Quantitative and qualitative methods are combined to capture performance trends and contextual factors — surveys, interviews, FGDs, and document review.
03
Analysis
Focus is on interpretation rather than description alone. Patterns, trends, disparities, and unintended effects are examined to explain results and identify drivers of performance.
04
Reporting & Use
Findings are synthesized into clear, actionable outputs that support decisions. Reports emphasize implications, recommendations, and options for adaptation.
Areas of Practice

Five Core
Domains

01
Program Evaluation
Assessing relevance, effectiveness, efficiency, and coherence of health programs in relation to their objectives and target populations — with a clear eye on what the evidence means for practice.
02
Impact Assessment
Examining short- and long-term outcomes to understand how interventions contribute to observed changes in health behaviors, service utilization, or system performance at community and facility levels.
03
Monitoring & Performance Analysis
Using routine data and indicators to track progress, identify risks, and guide timely corrective action — ensuring that programs stay on course and respond to emerging realities.
04
Evidence-Based Recommendations
Translating findings into practical actions that inform planning, resource allocation, and implementation strategies — bridging the gap between what data shows and what decision-makers need.
05
Capacity Building in Evaluation
Strengthening evaluation skills among teams and stakeholders to support sustainable monitoring, learning, and accountability — so that evidence use becomes embedded in program culture.

Field Evidence

Evaluation Snapshots

Selected examples from community, facility, and campaign-level evaluation work.

Prevention
Malaria Prevention Evaluation
ContextCommunity mosquito net distribution in high-burden areas
MethodsHousehold surveys, observation, routine health data
Key InsightOwnership was high, but consistent use lagged significantly
Findings supported redesign of follow-up visits and targeted behavior change messaging.
NCD · Awareness
NCD Awareness Campaign
ContextYouth and community outreach on NCD prevention
MethodsPre- and post-intervention surveys, referral tracking
InsightAwareness improved rapidly, but service uptake required system linkage
Results informed integration of screening days with outreach activities.
Facility Quality
Facility Service Quality Review
ContextPrimary health care facilities
MethodsData quality audits, staff interviews, register reviews
InsightReporting gaps driven by workflow constraints, not capacity alone
Informed adjustments to reporting timelines and supervision practices.
Immunization
Immunization Campaign Monitoring
ContextMass vaccination in underserved communities
MethodsDaily coverage tracking, field supervision, rapid feedback loops
InsightMissed children clustered around mobility and timing barriers
Data supported micro-planning adjustments during campaign implementation.
Community
Community Health Outreach Assessment
ContextCommunity-led health education activities
MethodsFocus group discussions, attendance tracking
InsightTrust and messenger credibility strongly influenced participation
Findings reinforced the role of local facilitators and peer educators.
Emergency Response
Emergency Health Response Review
ContextRapid response health interventions during emergency phases
MethodsRapid assessments, daily situation reports
InsightSpeed of coordination outweighed technical complexity as a success factor
Evidence supported simplified reporting tools during emergency phases — reducing burden and increasing real-time data use.
Data Systems
Hospital Data Systems Review
ContextFacility-level reporting and health information systems
MethodsData flow mapping, record reviews, staff consultations
InsightParallel reporting systems reduced data use at facility level
Supported streamlining of indicators and reporting responsibilities.
